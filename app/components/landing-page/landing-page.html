Cobudget makes it easy for organizations and groups to allocate funds collaboratively and transparently.

What is Cobudget?

The idea is simple: group members receive funds in their cobudget account. They can propose projects or activities and allocate their own funds to support projects or activities of others within the group.

Organizations Using Cobudget

Outlandish

Outlandish is a web services consultancy and worker-owned cooperative based in London.

How do they use Cobudget?

To democratically distribute their dividends to all people in the organization and make it easy for them to invest in work they care about.

What type of activities do they fund?

New tech products and platforms, social impact projects that need software, match funding of international initiatives.

Pre-Cobudget, there was a lack of transparency and the authority to spend surplus was limited.
- Brian Spurling, Co-Owner Outlandish

Institute for Evolutionary Leadership

A consulting company and community of thought leaders ready to redesign our world to make it more just, sustainable, and flourishing.

How do they use Cobudget?

To offer their member community a way to participate directly in the organization's financial decisions and empower them to fund each others workshops.

What type of activities do they fund?

Events such as workshops, travel expenses, systemic change work.

This technology is an enabler of new ways of doing things. Without it we would be using ugly spreadsheets.
- Fyodor Ovchinnikov, Co-Founder IEL
